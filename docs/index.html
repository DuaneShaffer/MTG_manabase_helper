The gap most lists ignore

Spells are the easy part. The mana is the math.

You picked twenty-three great cards and then sleeved up whatever lands tapped for the right colors. But a manabase either delivers your colors on the turn you cast a card — or it doesn't, and the game decides it for you.

Manabase answers the one question your list can't: given these lands, how often do you actually cast this card on curve?

What you get

Read your mana like the spells are already cast.

Card-by-card grades

Every colored spell gets a letter: the odds its colors are ready by the turn you cast it. The math is honest — it caps at 99%, never a fake guarantee.

The right land count

Not a rule of thumb. Your curve sets the number, trimmed for cheap card draw and ramp, clamped to where your archetype actually lives.

A base, recommended

One click fills the gaps — untapped sources first, taplands capped, every color you need covered, sized to your land target.

Simulate real games

Play out thousands of shuffles, mulligans and draws — mana screw and flood included — for the true on-curve odds a formula can't see.

Lands that think

Duals, choose-a-color lands, and conditional fixers that only count their colors when your deck actually turns them on.

Under the hood

The model, not a guess.

Requirements come straight from Frank Karsten's conditional hypergeometric model — the one competitive players cite — with the sliding (89 + mana value)% confidence target.

Then a Monte-Carlo simulator pressure-tests the build against thousands of real games, because a closed-form number reads rosy when half your lands enter tapped.
